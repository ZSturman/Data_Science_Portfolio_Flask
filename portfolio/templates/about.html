My Story:

What attracted me to data science the most was my skills with Blender , an opensource, 3D animation software which can be used for almost any and every need you may have with data visualization. I knew the math and the software, now I just had to learn the language.

After doing a bit of research I discovered that Python was the language for me. I jumped on YouTube and started watching tutorials from the basics to neuroscience computation and everything in between. Through plenty of trial and error I created a note-taking and studying strategy that worked for me.

Needing to still bring home a paycheck to survive I switched to part-time as a casino dealer and logged anywhere from 4 to 12 hours a day watching tutorials, reading documentation, creating projects and drinking lot's of coffee. After a year of confusion, pulling out my hair and screaming at my monitor, "Why isn't this working?!" I finally got to a point where I was ready to put myself into the 'ready-to-work' hiring pool and make it all look easy.



Competitive Advantage:

Many data scientists or data analyists today come from a bootcamp where they're taught how to proficiently complete school projects, but not much else. When out in the real world there are problems that arise which can only be solved with creative problem solving that coding bootcamps just can't teach.

With no syllabis, no professor and no course material to turn to I had to, not think outside the box, but create the box entirely from scratch which added more time to to my eventual "graduation" but allowed me to gather and develop skills I wouldn't possess otehrwise.
